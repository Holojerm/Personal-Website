Dr. Rosalind Franklin

Dr. Rosalind Franklin was a famous English chemist and pioneer x-ray crystallographer who unravelled the structure of DNA and RNA.

    A timeline of Dr. Franklin's life:

  • 1920 - Second child to Ellis Arthur Franklin and Muriel Frances Waley
  • 1938 - Receives a scholarship for higher studies
  • 1941 - Graduates from Newnham College of Cambridge University
  • 1942 - Became Assistant Research Officer at British Coal Utilisation Research Association
  • 1945 - Receives doctorate from Cambridge
  • 1947 - Takes position in Paris at 'Laboratoire Central des Services Chimiques de l'Etat'
  • 1950 - Obtains fellowing to work at King's College in London
  • 1951 - Finds that DNA 'B' is helical in structure
  • 1953 - Finds that both forms of DNA are comprised of two helical structures via Photograph 51. Joins Birkbeck College
  • 1955 - Publishes her findings on the tobacco mosaic virus (TMV), a single stranded RNA virus
  • 1956 - Discovers position and structure of RNA in TMV
  • 1958 - Passes away due to abdominal cancer

Her devotion to research showed itself at its finest in the last months of her life. Although stricken with an illness which she knew would be fatal, she continued to work right up to the end.

- John Desmond Bernal
